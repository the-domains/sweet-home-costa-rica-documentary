Sweet Home Costa Rica looks at a group of ordinary American families who, when faced with an intolerable threat to their religious freedom and antiwar beliefs, found the courage not only to stand up to the U.S. Government but to turn and walk away. They went farther than most. And they never looked back.

The film tells the story of eleven Quaker families from Alabama whose search for peace, religious freedom and a less materialistic environment in which to raise their children led them to a cloud forest in northwestern Costa Rica as beautiful as it was remote. There were no roads, no schools, no electricity. They named their new home Monteverde, literally "Green Mountain," and set about rebuilding their lives and putting their faith and pacifism into practice. Today Monteverde is home to a thriving Friends School (est. 1951) and several biological research institutes and is considered a model for sustainable ecotourism.
